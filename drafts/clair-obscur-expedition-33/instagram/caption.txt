CLAIR OBSCUR: EXPEDITION 33 — REVIEW ◆ 3/5
Never heard of it? Start at slide 1 — everything you need is inside. Swipe →

Every year, the Paintress paints a number — and everyone of that age is erased. The count is at 33, and Expedition 33 is Lumière's last, desperate answer.

A Belle Époque JRPG that plays like a painting scored by a live orchestra. Question Arcs, Answer Arcs, and an ending that hands you the brush — no True End, just a choice you'll keep debating long after the credits. Worthy of its Game of the Year win.

The catch? One well-timed parry nullifies everything — damage, danger, suspense. Fresh for 7 hours, hollow for the next 60. A QTE game disguised as a turn-based RPG.

Verdict: a grand opera of a game — breathtaking to watch, forgiving to a fault. Recommended for the narrative alone.

"For those who come after."
